Quick start

Up and running
in three steps

You don't need to configure anything. These three steps will get you using the extension properly from the first session.

01
Pin the extension to your toolbar

Click the puzzle-piece icon in your Chrome toolbar, find Chat Manager, and click the pin icon. It will appear next to your address bar permanently.

Chrome toolbar → Extensions → Pin
02
Open Claude and scroll your chat list

Visit claude.ai and scroll through your conversation history. Chat Manager reads the page and syncs your chats automatically — no manual import needed.

claude.ai → scroll to load your chats
03
Click the icon — your sidebar is ready

Click the Chat Manager icon to open it as a popup, or switch to sidebar mode in Settings for a persistent panel alongside Claude.

Settings → Display → Switch to Sidebar

Four things to try
in your first session

These are the features that change how Claude actually feels to use. Try them in order — each one builds on the last.

⚡
Save your first prompt

Open Chat Manager and go to Prompts. Add any system instruction or writing tone you use regularly. Next time you need it, one click puts it directly into Claude.

Try saving a code review format or a writing tone you use often. You'll reach for this daily.
Free · Unlimited
📁
Create your first folder

Click the + next to Folders in the sidebar. Name it after a project, a client, or a topic. Drag any conversation into it — or right-click a chat to assign it.

Start with 2–3 folders that map to your actual work. You can always rename them.
Free · Up to 5 folders
🏷️
Tag a conversation

Click the ⋯ on any conversation in your list to open its options. Assign a tag — conversations can have multiple tags and you can filter by any combination.

Tags work across folders. "research", "to-review", "important" — whatever your system is.
Free · Up to 20 tags
🔄
Turn on cloud sync

Sign in with Google in Settings and your entire folder structure, tags, and saved prompts will sync across every browser you have the extension installed on.

Your actual conversation content stays local. Only metadata and prompts are synced.
Pro feature
